## Key Conclusions

- Human sleep consists of multiple physiological stages, each supporting distinct biological and cognitive functions.
- Slow-Wave Sleep provides the optimal neurophysiological environment for consolidating hippocampus-dependent declarative memories.
- Memory consolidation is an active biological process driven by coordinated neural activity during sleep rather than passive storage after learning.
- Current evidence indicates that SWS and REM sleep contribute to memory processing through complementary mechanisms rather than competing functions.
