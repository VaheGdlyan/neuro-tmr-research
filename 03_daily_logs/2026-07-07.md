## Key Conclusion

The nervous system operates through the cooperation of two major cellular systems. Neurons are the primary information-processing units responsible for receiving, integrating, and transmitting signals, while glial cells maintain the biological environment that allows neuronal communication to function efficiently. Every higher brain function—including learning, memory, sleep, and cognition—ultimately depends on the interaction between these two cellular components.
