## Key Conclusion

Neuronal communication is a two-stage biological process. Information travels electrically within individual neurons as action potentials and is transferred chemically between neurons through neurotransmitters released at synapses. The balance between excitatory and inhibitory signaling enables stable communication across neural networks and forms the biological foundation of learning, memory, perception, and cognition.

## Product Implication

Targeted Memory Reactivation relies entirely on normal neuronal communication. Sensory cues presented during sleep can only influence memory if they activate the appropriate neural circuits through existing electrical and chemical signaling mechanisms. This understanding establishes the biological pathway through which external auditory cues may reactivate previously learned memories while maintaining the stability of the sleeping brain.
