### RQ-005 — Neural Information Encoding

- Consolidated previous research on how information is represented in the brain.
- Confirmed that memories are encoded as **distributed patterns of activity** across neural networks rather than individual neurons.
- Reviewed the central role of the **hippocampus** in encoding new declarative memories.
- Connected neuronal communication with large-scale neural network activity.
- Completed and documented the scientific answer for RQ-005.

---

## Key Conclusions

- Neurons communicate through a combination of electrical signaling within cells and chemical signaling between cells.
- Information is represented by coordinated activity across interconnected neural networks rather than isolated neurons.
- The hippocampus serves as the primary structure responsible for organizing and encoding new declarative memories before they become integrated into distributed cortical networks.
- Neural communication provides the biological mechanism that enables learning, memory formation, and future memory reactivation during sleep.

## Product Implications

Today's work established the biological communication pathway that underlies the future TMR system.

The project will not attempt to directly modify memories. Instead, it will aim to influence existing neural networks by delivering sensory cues at precisely timed moments during sleep. Successful memory reactivation depends entirely on normal neuronal communication and coordinated activity across distributed brain networks.

This understanding strengthens the scientific foundation for the product and prepares the transition toward studying memory consolidation and sleep-dependent learning.
