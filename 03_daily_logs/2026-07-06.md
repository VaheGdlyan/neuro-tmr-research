## Key Conclusion

Learning, memory, sensory processing, and sleep emerge from the coordinated activity of multiple interconnected brain regions rather than a single anatomical structure. The hippocampus, cerebral cortex, thalamus, hypothalamus, brainstem, amygdala, and cerebellum each contribute distinct functions that together form the biological foundation of Targeted Memory Reactivation (TMR).
