## Key Conclusions

- Sleep is an actively regulated physiological state rather than a period of brain inactivity.
- Human sleep consists of two distinct biological states—NREM and REM—that alternate throughout the night.
- Each sleep state has characteristic patterns of brain activity and physiological behavior.
- Sleep onset is a gradual transition that cannot be identified using a single physiological signal alone.
- The sleeping brain remains functionally active and continues to process certain biologically meaningful sensory information.
- Sleep provides the biological environment in which essential neural and physiological processes occur.
