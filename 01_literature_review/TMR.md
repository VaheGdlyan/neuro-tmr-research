# **Promoting Memory Consolidation During Sleep: A Meta-Analysis of Targeted Memory Reactivation**


**Authors:** [Xiaoqing Hu, et al. (Ken Paller's Lab)] | **Year:** [2020]

**Reason for Selection:**
For the Targeted Memory Reactivation (TMR) phase of this project, I selected the 2020 meta-analysis by Hu et al. as the primary source of truth because it aggregates the results of 91 independent TMR experiments, providing a statistically verified consensus of the entire field rather than relying on the isolated biases of a single study. From an engineering and data science perspective, utilizing a massive consolidated dataset is far more reliable and time-efficient than reading outdated textbooks or single clinical trials. Furthermore, this specific paper explicitly isolates the exact variables needed to program the system, comparing the effectiveness of audio cues across different sleep stages and memory types to define the direct constraints required to build a functional TMR product.
